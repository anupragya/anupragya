# *Hi, I'm Anupragya Chaudhary. 👋*

I sometimes wonder if engineering found me, or if I accidentally clicked "Enroll" one day and decided to commit to the bit.

I'm currently pursuing Computer Science, spending most of my days teaching computers what to do, only to realize they take instructions *very* literally. Somewhere between debugging Python, experimenting with AI, and convincing my code to cooperate, I discovered that I genuinely enjoy solving problems—even when they make me question every life choice that led me to a missing semicolon.

The funny thing is, I never fit neatly into one box. I'm an engineering student who loves literature, someone who enjoys building projects but also appreciates well-written stories. One day I'm training a machine learning model; the next I'm writing event scripts, designing presentations, or finding the perfect sentence that says exactly what I mean. Turns out creativity and logic get along better than people give them credit for.

Most of what you'll find here started as curiosity. Some projects were built because I wanted to learn something new. Others existed because a college assignment refused to let me sleep. A few worked on the first try—which is suspicious enough that I still don't fully trust them.

I'm currently exploring Python, data analytics, machine learning, and software development while trying to write cleaner code than I did yesterday. Progress, I've learned, looks less like overnight success and more like hundreds of tiny improvements that eventually add up.

Outside of code, I enjoy public speaking, organizing events, and occasionally pretending that planning everything in advance means life will actually follow the schedule. It never does, but optimism is free.

If you're browsing my repositories, you'll probably notice a mix of experiments, college projects, and ideas that seemed brilliant at 11 PM. Some are polished, some are works in progress, and a few exist simply because I wanted to see if I could build them.

I believe the best projects don't just demonstrate what you've learned—they quietly reveal how you think.

### Fun Fact ☕

I spend my days teaching computers to think logically and my evenings getting emotionally attached to fictional characters. Somehow, both hobbies make perfect sense.

So, welcome to my little corner of GitHub. Stay for the code, forgive the bugs, and if you find something interesting, let's build something even better.

*"Every great project starts with a simple idea and a dangerous amount of confidence."*
